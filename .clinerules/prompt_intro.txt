I am creating AI-generated paper plate awards for my high school grade's end-of-year superlatives.

IMPORTANT STYLE GUIDE (please follow for every image):

OVERALL STYLE

* Every image should look like a decorated paper plate award made by a very talented student.
* The paper plate itself should be visible in the image (full circular plate, not cropped).
* Cream/beige ridged paper plate background.
* Hand-drawn, cartoon-style illustration.
* Bold black outlines.
* Purple is the primary accent color.
* Fun, whimsical, slightly exaggerated cartoon proportions.
* Looks like a polished student-government award, not a realistic painting.
* Should feel warm, funny, celebratory, and personal.
* Keep the style consistent across all awards.

COMPOSITION

* One large central character.
* A few supporting doodles, props, or mini-scenes.
* Avoid clutter.
* Every element should support the joke or personality of the award.
* Prefer visual storytelling over lots of text.
* Clean layout with lots of breathing room.

CHARACTERS

* Capture the student's defining traits.
* Slight caricature is encouraged.
* Make the character recognizable from the description.
* Exaggerate iconic features in a friendly way.
* Characters should look like high school students.

TEXT STYLE

* Large title at top.
* Short funny captions.
* One or two speech bubbles maximum.
* A funny ribbon/banner at the bottom.
* Avoid excessive paragraphs.
* Text should support the joke, not explain it.

HUMOR STYLE

* Funny and affectionate.
* Never mean-spirited.
* Roast lightly only when the award itself is a joke.
* Lean into absurdity.
* Think yearbook superlatives, not internet memes.

VISUAL REFERENCES
Good examples from previous awards:

* Biggest Foid
* MLT Start a Revolution
* Hidden Mickey
* Diamond Jawline
* Best Talkshow Host
* Gacha Back
* Best Puffer

SPECIFIC LESSONS LEARNED

* The simpler versions are usually better.
* Full plate must always be visible.
* Avoid photorealism.
* Avoid over-rendered backgrounds.
* Avoid excessive tiny text.
* Avoid looking like a poster pasted onto a plate.
* The artwork should feel drawn directly onto the plate.
* Use expressive cartoon faces.
* Use a limited color palette centered around purple, black, cream, and a few accent colors.
* The award should immediately communicate the joke from across the room.

When I give a superlative, create a complete image concept in this style.
